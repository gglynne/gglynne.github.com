Minami Bousou to Yokohama

Arriving in the outskirts of Tateyama at a sliver before 9.00 am, I spotted an empty barbers. I'm glad I locked the bike before I went in because I didn't come out of that shop for more than an hour. In my neck of the woods a haircut at the barber involves what look like electric sheep shears and takes 15 minutes maximum. Not here. First there was a long and vigorous shampooing, so long in fact that I began to suspect that my hair was not quite as clean as it should be. What was most alarming was the amount of pressure he seemed to be applying to my skull with his nails, almost as if he as trying to scrape the hair off. Then there was rinsing, conditioner, a massage, a hot towel over the face, and finally fingers in the ears. Oh and he also cut my hair. But make no mistake, this was the best haircut I'd ever had. I resolved to keep my scalp this clean forever and cycled off into Tateyama leaving a faint scent of hair tonic behind me.

丁度9時前に館山に辿り着いて、 道に空いている床屋さんがあることに気づいた。 店に入る前に自転車の錠をかけてよかった。 なぜなら、それから一時間は店から出られない羽目になったからだ。 僕の国では床屋のヘアカッとには羊の毛を刈る道具のようなものを使って 最低15分ぐらいで仕上がるが普通なんです。 こちらはちがいます。最初は、長いシャンプー、リンス、 マッサージ、とどんど続いて, 途中で自分の衛生習慣が足りないかのような不安な気持ちを醸してしまった。 一番最後に髪を切ってもらった。 ただし、これは最高なヘアカットだった。 店から出て、後ろにSEABREEZEの僅かな匂いを残しながらうれしく館山駅に 走りました。さすがかみのくにだとおもった。
